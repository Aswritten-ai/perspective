Narrative SeparationOfConcerns

10 nodes in this area of the perspective

This hub collects architectural decisions about what belongs where — which tools do which jobs, and what must be kept apart. On the tool-boundary side: [compile should never accept a focus parameter](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#Claim_CompileNoFocusParam), preserving full-worldview grounding; [introspect should not return subgraph content alongside its analysis](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#Claim_IntrospectNoSubgraphReturn), since the two serve different moments; and [absence as a lens dimension was collapsed into introspect](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#Claim_AbsenceIsIntrospect) rather than kept separate. Moving the other direction, [scope was designated a new, distinct tool](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#Claim_ScopeIsNewSeparateTool) — windowed graph to full depth — effectively deprecating worldview domain layers. On the lens side: [the lens tool generates lenses rather than running them](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#Claim_LensToolIsGenerator), [a lens is compound rather than stacked](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#Claim_LensIsCompoundNotStacked), and [eye chart and text refraction are mechanically identical operations](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#Claim_TextAndEyeChartSameOperation) — one saved, one ad-hoc. The [multi-pass extraction pipeline](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#Narrative_MultiPassExtractionPipeline) (dated Jul 2026) draws a related distinction: passes are compute stages, transactions are atomic writes. A [JavaScript spread-order bug fix](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#Claim_Base64SpreadOrderBug) sits as a concrete implementation artifact within this otherwise architectural cluster.
