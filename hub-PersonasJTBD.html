The persona and Job-to-be-Done (JTBD) model has evolved from a four-archetype set—[Knowledge Packager] (Archetype_KnowledgePackager), [Trust Seeker] (Archetype_TrustSeeker), [Tinkerer] (Archetype_Tinkerer), and [Knowledge-Rich Consumer] (Archetype_KnowledgeRichConsumer)—into a [three-axis taxonomy] (Narrative_ArchetypeTaxonomyRestructured) defined by expertise, context, and consumption. This shift categorizes users as the [expert who distributes] (Position_ArchetypeExpertDistributes), the [leader who delegates] (Position_ArchetypeLeaderDelegates), or the [professional who works with AI daily] (Position_ArchetypeProfessionalDailyAI).

Underlying these models is the observation that the primary B2C job is [providing structure for context and memory] (Obs_StructureContextMemory). However, the taxonomy faces internal friction; a recent reclassification of a [beta user from outside tech] (Archetype_KnowledgeRichConsumer) highlighted a [typology axis collision] (Position_TypologyAxisCollision) between AI-fluency and distribution-motion. Furthermore, the founder's own usage patterns serve as a [cautionary research baseline] (Position_FounderAsPoorUserExample), as typical users may not maintain their graphs with the same rigor. Strategic focus is currently directed toward [architecture and standards teams] (Narrative_ArchitectureTeamsBeachhead) as an ideal beachhead, with [operations personnel] (StyleObs_OpsPersonaQuote) identified as a high-pain target for pitch development.
