Narrative NavigationReplacesEmbeddedSnapshot

6 nodes in this area of the perspective

The architectural shift toward navigation-first interaction, initiated in mid-June 2026, centers on replacing monolithic snapshots with a [digest-driven traversal model](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#Position_NavigationDropsContextOrderOfMagnitude). This move, which reduced per-call context by an order of magnitude, aligns with [independent industry parallels](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#Position_DiscoveryConsultantHATEOASParallel) that favor link-based discovery over large-scale queries. By July 2026, this philosophy matured into a [standardized perspective response](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#Narrative_PerspectiveResponseOrderedByVolatility) structured by volatility, ensuring that stable maps remain constant while per-call focus is relegated to the end. To maintain graph integrity, the system adopted [synchronous per-message integration](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#Narrative_SynchronousPerMessageIntegration), rejecting asynchronous patterns to prevent state drift. The user experience now mirrors this machine-centric logic, with the [digest browser](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#Narrative_DigestBrowserProductSurface) serving as the primary interface for both human and agent. Finally, the team has defined a [content/commentary split](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#Narrative_ContentCommentarySplit) to distinguish between raw source material and authored reconciliation, though the write-side implementation remains a pending task.
