Narrative PerspectiveVsInformation

4 nodes in this area of the perspective

This hub argues the load-bearing distinction for modules versus RAG: modules deliver *perspective*, not information. The mainline holds that [AIs already carry a singular opinion] (Claim_AIHasSingularOpinion) buried in the middle of their training set, which modules replace with curated, attributed perspective. The [perspective-not-information thesis] (Claim_PerspectiveNotInformation) frames this as the exact gap between a human expert and an AI — loading narratives, approaches, and framings about what information *means*, not just the information. Two children extend the case along different axes. A [later external-validation note (Apr 14)] (Claim_CustomerCTOAISlopConcern) recasts perspective defensively, as the guardrail against AI slop, citing the customer's CTO's own articulation of the problem. Separately, the [worldview-encoding-as-behavior claim] (Claim_WorldviewEncodesAIBehavior) shifts ground from epistemics to AI behavioral tics ('good catch', collaborative flatness). The cluster is dated Mar 19–22 with the the customer's CTO signal arriving later, so the framing has accreted rather than resolved.
