MarketContext

5 nodes in this area of the perspective

This hub gathers the framings of the market problem the venture addresses, all circling a single root — AI tooling starved of shared organizational context. The [context-starvation thesis] (Opportunity_ADR002) holds that coding, executive, and content domains each run on separate tools with no shared context, producing strategy-execution gaps; the [fragmentation problem] (Narrative_FragmentationProblem) names the same disconnect from the developer's side. The [context-quality angle] (Narrative_SIC_Opportunity_1) reframes it as output quality: high-quality AI output needs extensive context, which models currently approximate via search. Two children escalate the stakes differently. The [IP crisis] (Narrative_IPCrisis) narrates a paradigm shift from developer convenience to a threat-level, existential business problem — employees leaking secrets, models absorbing IP into training data. The [onboarding crisis] (Opportunity_OnboardingCrisis) quantifies it instead: [amount]+/year in fragmentation costs, framed as pain felt now by every CEO. Read together they span problem (fragmentation, starvation) and stakes (existential risk vs. quantified present cost), without converging on one framing.
