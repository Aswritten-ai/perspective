StyleMetrics

14 nodes in this area of the perspective

This hub gathers per-document style and readability metrics across the org's corpus, computed on shared axes: active-voice ratio, jargon density, lexical diversity (TTR), sentence-length variance, and — where given — Flesch-Kincaid grade. The dominant profile is consistent: high active voice, domain-appropriate jargon, varied sentence length, strong lexical diversity. Register splits clearly by document type: technical architecture pieces — [collective-memory addendum] (Metrics_cm_addendum), [collective-memory arch] (Metrics_collective_memory_arch), and [SIC transcript] (StyleMetrics_SIC_1) — run high jargon density; pitch, GTM, CVP, and rebrand material lean concise and accessible. The [GTM doc] (StyleMetrics_GTM) and [CVP] (StyleMetrics_collective_memory_cvp) exemplify the punchy, short-sentence end of the spectrum; the [ADR001] (Metrics_ADR001) and [IP-crisis strategy] (Metrics_IPCrisis) anchor the denser, more complex end. The [founder reflection] (StyleMetrics_founder_reflection) is the sole child with a full numeric profile — 80 sentences averaging 42.8 words each, 78% active voice, TTR 0.62 — making it the most precisely witnessed node. Most other entries are qualitative-only. The [deck-editing notes] (StyleMetrics_DeckEditing) and [customer architect transcript] (Metrics_CustomerArchitect) both exhibit mixed cadence: short punchy sentences alongside longer analytical or explanatory clauses.
