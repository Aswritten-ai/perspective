StyleMetrics

17 nodes in this area of the perspective

This hub gathers per-document style and readability metrics across the org's corpus, computed on the same axes: active-voice ratio, jargon density, lexical diversity (TTR), sentence-length variance, and — where given — Flesch-Kincaid grade. The dominant profile is consistent: high active voice, domain-appropriate-but-explained jargon, varied sentence length, strong lexical diversity. FK grades cluster college-level, spanning the conversational [the customer's chief architect transcript at 10.2] (Metrics_CustomerArchitect) and [deck-editing notes near 10] (StyleMetrics_DeckEditing) up through the [B2B pitch at 11.2] (Metrics_1) and the denser [ADR] (Metrics_ADR001) / [IP-crisis strategy docs at 12.4] (Metrics_IPCrisis). Register splits by document type: technical architecture pieces ([collective-memory addendum] (Metrics_cm_addendum), [arch] (Metrics_collective_memory_arch), [SIC] (StyleMetrics_SIC_1)) run high jargon density; pitch, GTM, CVP, and rebrand material lean concise and accessible. Many entries are qualitative-only; only a subset carry numeric FK. The corpus is not uniform on cadence or sentence length — see the variation below.
