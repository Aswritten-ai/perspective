Conviction

This hub gathers the conviction construct: the epistemic settledness of organizational knowledge, formalized as a [Notion→Stake→Boulder→Grave framework] (Narrative_ConvictionFramework) where memories raise or lower conviction with provenance for every shift. Conviction is repeatedly defined as *built over time through agreement and action* — Scarlet's [canonical distinction] (Obs_ConvictionTemporalityQuote) anchors this. Two design tensions run through the children. First, the pipeline [conflates conviction with temporality] (Claim_ConvictionAndTemporalityAreDistinctAxes) — *when* a claim applies (now/soon/future/speculative) is orthogonal to *how settled* it is, a [distinct-axes argument] (Narrative_ConvictionVsTemporality) validated by the customer's chief architect's demo-pollution failure (Apr–May 2026). Second, conviction is treated as separate from [review evidence] (Claim_5) — extraction-validation thoroughness — which informs compile behavior independently. A live feature request proposes [conviction decay] (Claim_ConvictionDecayFeatureRequest): a founder beta user's [spaced-repetition framing] (Narrative_ConvictionDecay) where unreinforced claims lose weight. Claim_5 also extends the dynamic to human organizations. A stylistic trace records [BOULDER as an all-caps inline label] (StyleObs_ConvictionLabel).
