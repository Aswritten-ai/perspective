This hub gathers how aswritten models *who occupies what* in an organization. The mainline distinction is firm and dated: a [role is an abstraction with its own module] (Narrative_OrgRolesAbstraction) that persists independently of the person filling it (Mar 2026, from PR 91), while an [actor is a specific person] (Narrative_ActorVsRoleAbstraction) — Tony, then a demo-call prospect — where multiple actors contribute to one role over time and assignment emerges from conversation, not static tags (Apr 2026). [Roles are first-class product concepts] (Claim_OrgRolesFirstClass), and the team itself is [staffed as perspectives on cadence] (Claim_TeamAsPerspectives) rather than full-time hires — Scarlet [needs no co-founder but a constellation] (Claim_NoCoFounderNeeded) (Mar 2026). A grounding case: the [practitioner occupies three transaction shapes at once] (Narrative_PractitionerThreeRoles) (supplier, customer, trust-broker) from one account (Apr 2026). Running underneath since May 2025 is the [Microsoft gatekeeper pattern] (Claim_MultiRoleCoordinationGatekeeper) — a centralized ground-truth mechanism flagged as *missing* — which by Apr 2026 [surfaces unresolved design questions] (Claim_GatekeeperDesignQuestionsOpen) rather than an answer. Concrete sales-role mechanics ([Tony influence vs a demo-call prospect direct] (Obs_DemoProspectVsTonyDivision)) ground the abstraction.
