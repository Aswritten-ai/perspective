SalesDecks

24 nodes in this area of the perspective

This hub tracks the evolution of sales-as-delivery, where the act of selling is indistinguishable from the product itself. Early requests for [pre-read decks] (Position_PreReadDeckRequest) and [written sales guides] (Position_SalesGuideRequest) (Mar 2026) quickly shifted toward high-fidelity, visual-first demonstrations. The trajectory moved from static artifacts to [GIF-based materials] (Narrative_GIFBasedSalesMaterials) that simulate real product usage, exemplified by the [before/after money GIF] (Narrative_DemoGIFStory) and a [finalized four-step demo script] (Narrative_DemoScriptOutsideTechBetaUser).

Central to this is the [podcast/interview as sales motion] (Narrative_PodcastInterviewSalesMotion), which collapses demo, onboarding, and content into one event. This serves as a [forcing function] (Narrative_InterviewForcingFunction) for human-driven development, ensuring product features remain reactionary to actual user needs. Tony Maley further extended this by proposing a [procedural sales motion] (Narrative_SellTheProcessPreCaseStudy) to engage enterprise prospects before formal case studies exist. The current emphasis is on [generosity as demonstration] (Narrative_GenerosityAsDemonstration), where unsolicited bespoke artifacts delivered within 48 hours of a call act as the primary conversion engine, replacing traditional pitch-based models with a [keep-it-running] (Narrative_KeepingBeatsStarting) conversion ask.
