Calibration

11 nodes in this area of the perspective

Calibration gathers how aswritten validates its own quality — the recurring frame is optometric. The [optometrist model] (Narrative_OptometristModel) and [lens-based review] (Narrative_LensBasedReview) cast testing as swapping lenses over a constant target: an [eye chart] (Claim_8) is a fixed-format document where \"lens changes, eye chart stays constant,\" and convergence is judged by the idiom [\"Better through A or B?\"] (StyleObs_BetterThroughAOrB). A second strand insists quality is earned over time, not asserted: [soak testing] (Narrative_SoakTestQuality) holds that validation \"requires sustained real-world use, not unit tests,\" and the [single-node extract] (Claim_SoakTestRequired) needs days/weeks of living-with-it. A third strand is about *where* you test: Scarlet flagged (Mar 19) a [clean-sandbox need] (Claim_CleanSandboxNeeded) against a \"dirty env,\" and (Mar 16) argued a [beta user's repo] (Claim_BetaRepoAsCleanTestEnv) is the clean environment since the org's tool density conflates behaviors. Distinct in timescale, the [Thursday 5/7 demo] (Narrative_ThursdayDemoAsDiagnostic) reframes a chatbot showing as a low-cost diagnostic of stakeholder appetite (May 1). Style observations sit alongside as related craft notes.
