RhetoricalQuestion

14 nodes in this area of the perspective

This hub collects style observations on the rhetorical question as a recurring device across the org's writing. The dominant pattern is the opening hook: a question that universalizes a fear or pain before the pivot — the [hallucination-fear hook] (StyleObs_RhetoricalQuestion), the [trust-binary opener] (StyleObs_RhetoricalHook), and the [shared-pain second-person open] (StyleObs_RhetoricalQ_1) all run problem-agitate-solve cadence. A distinct branch uses questions as epistemic reframing rather than persuasion: the [lens-not-facts antithesis] (StyleObs_NotFacts), the [optometrist 'A or B?' idiom] (StyleObs_BetterThroughAOrB), and [is-this-the-right-road] (StyleObs_RhetoricalQ). A third branch turns questions into conviction/scope boundaries — the [falsifiable closing hypothesis] (StyleObs_11), the [guardrails open question] (StyleObs_7), and the [Open Questions section] (StyleObs_OpenQuestions). Idiomatic, British-inflected voice ([How mad is that?] (StyleObs_HowMadRhetQ)) and implicit section-header questions ([Why YC] (StyleObs_WhyYC), [Key Insight] (StyleObs_KeyInsightCallout)) round out the span.
