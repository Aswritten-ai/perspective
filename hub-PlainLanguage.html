This hub gathers observations of Scarlet's plain-language style across founder-facing documents. The dominant thread is an accessibility standard stated as an [absolute imperative] (StyleObs_TamarackReview_PlainLanguage) on 2026-03-13: no jargon unless defined first. Two mechanisms recur. One is outright [jargon exclusion] (StyleObs_PlainLanguagePolicy) — no git, no MCP, no RDF — describing the pipeline in two sentences. The other is gloss-in-place: [inline parentheticals] (StyleObs_ParentheticalClarification) like '(that's scope)', the organic [branch-and-trunk analogy] (StyleObs_BranchTrunkAnalogy) for version control, and [plain explanation] (StyleObs_ExtractionPlainLanguage) of backend extraction. Voice traits reinforce it: [first-person, active, alternating] (StyleObs_9) long sentences with short verdicts ('Cents, not dollars.'), [triadic momentum and high-stakes declaration] (StyleObs_7), [colloquial catch-alls] (StyleObs_DirectVoice) ('All of that'), and a [quantified value prop] (StyleObs_RatioValueProp) using a ratio with no dollar anchor. Note the counter-current: the same observer flags deliberate retention of precise terms — 'navigates' vs 'queries', 'FOUNDATION' all-caps as semantic weight — so plainness coexists with chosen technical precision rather than erasing it.
