WhatDoesItDo

22 nodes in this area of the perspective

This hub answers \"what does it do\" in two registers that pull in different directions. The product spine: As Written is a [git-native organizational memory tool] (Observation_AsWrittenProduct) that compiles a worldview graph and exposes MCP tools; upstream it [extracts undocumented expert knowledge] (Observation_WhatDoesItDo_1) through conversational interviews; downstream it proves itself through a three-beat demo — [cold delegation] (Observation_Beat1_ColdDelegation) (real work with no briefing), a [citation layer] (Observation_Beat2_CitationLayer) tracing claims to conversations, and [gap honesty] (Observation_Beat3_GapHonesty) (it says what it doesn't know and who to ask). The demo's emotional core is the [user querying their own worldview] (Narrative_MemoryAsWitness) in real time. The V1 interface thesis: [MCP endpoint plus notifications, GitHub as infrastructure not interface] (Obs_V1UXVision), with [ambient capture inside Claude Code] (StyleObs_AmbientQuote). A large second register is style/craft analysis of how the pitch itself is written — bold beat markers, imperative call-flow, extraction-vs-retrieve verb discipline. The two registers (what the product does vs. how the pitch performs) coexist here rather than resolve.
