This hub collects observations about how individual speakers sound — their idiolects, register markers, and the editorial conventions that preserve or compress them. The dominant thread is Scarlet's voice. [The conversational phrasing rule](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#Narrative_ConversationalPhrasingRule) (Jun 2026) holds that her multi-clause, filler-heavy spoken register must be preserved verbatim in outreach material; coined compressions are rejected as AI-sounding because they fail the test of whether she can say them aloud on a call. [The founding rejection event](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#Obs_ScarletRejectsAIPhrasing) grounds this rule, and [the positive exemplar](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#StyleObs_ConversationalVsCompressed) — three-verb enumeration, no nominalization — shows what the preferred register looks like in practice. Across session memos, Scarlet's blockquoted speech is consistently run-on, lowercase, and filler-heavy: [the onboarding memo](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#StyleObs_OnboardingMethodology_BlockquoteVoice), [the protocol memo](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#StyleObs_Protocol_VerbatimBlockquotes), [the practitioner beta memo](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#StyleObs_PractitionerBetaUserBlockquoteRegister), and [the vision-frame session](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#StyleObs_VisionFrameDirectQuote) all exhibit the same dual-register structure: structured editorial prose surrounding unedited conversational blockquotes. [Idiolect preservation is an explicit editorial policy](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#StyleObs_ForgettingBottleneck_DirectQuote) in at least one memory, with the rationale stated directly. [The fundraising brief](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#StyleObs_FundraisingBriefReview_DirectQuoteAnchor) extends this pattern: the founder's verbatim speech functions as the conviction marker, elevating claims from editorial assertion to attributed decision. One outlier: [the verbatim blockquote from Jun 2026](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#StyleObs_VerbatimBlockquote) notes Scarlet's quoted speech is unusually aphoristic and axiom-like in that memory, borrowed from philosophical argument rather than the typical stream-of-consciousness cadence. A [Jul 2026 voice memo](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#StyleObs_MoonshotVoiceMemoRegister) captures raw stream-of-consciousness before it went stale, explicitly flagging the deliberate choice to preserve unedited register. Other speakers are catalogued distinctly: [Tony](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#StyleObs_DirectSpeechPlaybook) is directive and filler-heavy, shorter and more declarative than Scarlet; [a consultant with fifteen years of enterprise discovery experience](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#StyleObs_DiscoveryConsultantCallRegister) draws on RFC/IETF vocabulary and historical computing metaphors; [a collaborator's talk transcript](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#StyleObs_AgentSim_VerbatimBlockquotes) preserves colloquial filler-heavy speech contrasting with analytical editorial prose; and [the customer's chief architect](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#StyleObs_TemporalityValidation_BlockquoteVoice) is self-interrupting and filler-heavy. A structurally distinct outlier is [the identity-as-architecture observation](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#Observation_5), which frames identity as downstream of narrative context via an Enneagram lens — thematically adjacent but not a register or idiolect observation. [StyleObs_10](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#StyleObs_10) adds document-level meta-transparency markers — explicit conviction and confidence labels, 'compression anxiety' as a coined compound — that sit at the boundary between idiolect and editorial convention.
