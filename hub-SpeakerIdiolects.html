46 nodes in this area of the perspective

This hub collects observations about how individual speakers sound — their idiolects, register markers, and the editorial conventions that preserve or compress them. The dominant thread is Scarlet's voice: [the conversational phrasing rule](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#Narrative_ConversationalPhrasingRule) (established Jun 2026) holds that her multi-clause, filler-heavy spoken register must be preserved verbatim in outreach material, with coined compressions rejected as AI-sounding. [The founding rejection event](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#Obs_ScarletRejectsAIPhrasing) grounds this rule, and [the positive exemplar](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#StyleObs_ConversationalVsCompressed) — three-verb enumeration, no nominalization — shows what the preferred register looks like in practice. Across session memos, [Scarlet's blockquoted speech](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#StyleObs_OnboardingMethodology_BlockquoteVoice) is consistently run-on, lowercase, and filler-heavy, contrasting with tighter editorial prose — a [dual-register structure](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#StyleObs_EmbeddedVerbatimQuotes) documented as the canonical memo format. [Idiolect preservation is an explicit editorial policy](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#StyleObs_ForgettingBottleneck_DirectQuote) in at least one memory. Other speakers are catalogued distinctly: [the customer's chief architect](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#StyleObs_ARB_BlockquoteVerbatim) is self-interrupting and filler-heavy with a minimizing register for positive signals; [Tony](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#StyleObs_DirectSpeechPlaybook) is directive and filler-heavy; [a consultant who ran enterprise discovery engagements for fifteen years](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#StyleObs_DiscoveryConsultantCallRegister) draws on RFC/IETF vocabulary and historical computing metaphors. A newer [rich-transcription format](https://aswritten.ai/narrative#StyleObs_VoiceMemoDeliveryAnnotations) (Jul 2026) adds per-span acoustic annotations — rate, pitch, energy, pause — enabling paralinguistic extraction beyond content alone.
