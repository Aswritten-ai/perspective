This hub gathers the product's tool surface and capability decisions, mostly dated March–May 2026. The dominant arc is aggressive consolidation: a March–April sweep settled the [five user-facing MCP tools] (Claim_FiveCoreMCPTools) — perspective, cite, introspect, remember, review — while [refract and its registry] (Claim_RefractRegistryRemovedFromProduct), the [memories/source tool] (Claim_MemoriesSourceToolRemoved), [discoveries] (Claim_RemoveDiscoveries), the [notifications system] (Claim_RemoveNotificationsSystem), and [auto-generated TX summaries] (Claim_TXSummariesAutoGenRemoved) were removed; scope and lens folded inward. The [review tool was widened to three cases] (Claim_ReviewToolThreeCases). Capability framing runs alongside: [compilation layers] (Obs_CompilationLayers) from 4K bootstrap to 100K expansion, [perspective layering] (Obs_ChineseHerbalismLayeringExample), [transcript bootstrapping] (Claim_TranscriptBootstrapOnramp) as a lighter onramp, and a [public perspective module] (Narrative_PublicPerspectiveModule). Against the removal current, the [Feedback/Help tool] (Claim_FeedbackHelpAboveLine) was pulled *above* the line as primary churn prevention. Early usage (May) showed [two organic loop patterns] (Claim_FourToolLoopEngagedUserPattern) across the enterprise customer's first nine days.
