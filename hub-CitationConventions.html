CitationConventions

13 nodes in this area of the perspective

This hub documents the evolving citation conventions used to ground claims in source material. The primary evidentiary register relies on [timestamp-anchored speaker attribution] (StyleObs_TimestampedSpeakerAttribution), which provides temporal provenance for spoken evidence. While [MM:SS] (StyleObs_ConversationalTimestamps) remains the standard, variations including [HH:MM:SS] (StyleObs_DavidNolen_TimestampCitations) and [inline grounding] (StyleObs_TimestampGrounding) are employed to maintain traceability. Parallel to these temporal markers, the perspective utilizes a [two-register document structure] (StyleObs_NicheBlockquoteVerbatim) that separates [verbatim blockquote] (StyleObs_ThreeUseCases_VerbatimBlock) evidence from editorial synthesis. This structure is driven by the requirement that [precision matters] (StyleObs_VerbatimPrecision) when capturing technical or taxonomic claims. A distinct, non-temporal convention involves [backtick-wrapped IRIs] (StyleObs_NotNegotiationFloor), which function as an idiolect for cross-referencing internal graph entities. Together, these methods ensure that claims are either anchored to a specific moment in a recording or explicitly demarcated as authoritative verbatim content.
