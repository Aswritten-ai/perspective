The trajectory of aswritten’s market positioning has shifted from a binary choice between B2B and B2C toward a unified model of individual-led growth. Following the initial [B2C primary-market convergence] (Position_B2CPrimaryMarket) in March 2026, the animating thesis held that [AI has made every individual their own team] (Position_EveryIndividualIsTheirOwnTeam), effectively turning the solopreneur into the primary unit of value. This was complemented by the observation that [AI has restored individual contributor status] (Position_AIRestoredICStatus), shifting the professional focus back to strategy and curation. While an [open question] (Position_SMBPivotReconciliationNeeded) in April highlighted friction between this volume-B2C pivot and an enterprise-style engagement pattern, Scarlet later proposed that [Expert, Team, and Org are a continuum of one use case] (Narrative_TierContinuumNotDistinctMarkets). This framework asserts that organizational tiers do not supersede the B2C framing; rather, the solo Expert tier serves as the natural on-ramp. Most recently, the pivot has been grounded in [founder-fit] (Position_FounderFitNarrativeEdge), with the recognition that [B2B defaults were career artifacts] (Position_B2BDefaultCareerArtifact) rather than strategic requirements. The current operational focus is the [individuation curve] (Narrative_IndividuationCurveConversionMechanic), mapping how users progress from generic engagement to ownership.
