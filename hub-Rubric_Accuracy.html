This hub tracks accuracy assessments across the corpus, evaluating factual precision, named-entity integrity, and citation discipline. Technical documentation, including [collective-memory] (Assess_Accuracy_collective_memory) and [code-memory] (Assess_Accuracy_cm) assessments, emphasizes precise type signatures, SHACL schemas, and explicit correction logging. Strategic and GTM materials, such as the [GTM rubric] (RubricAssess_Accuracy_GTM) and [investor-facing assessments] (RubricAssess_Accuracy_1), are scrutinized for quantified metrics and alignment with product architecture. Decision records ([ADR-001] (RubricAssess_Accuracy_ADR001), [ADR-002] (RubricAssess_Accuracy_ADR002)) focus on cross-reference consistency and status clarity. The [IP-crisis entry] (Assessment_Accuracy_IPCrisis) and [customer architect call] (Assessment_Accuracy_CustomerArchitect) highlight the preservation of verbatim quotes and the explicit labeling of exploratory or uncommitted claims. Broad [foundational rubrics] (RubricAssess_Accuracy) validate internal consistency and entity naming, though they occasionally note the absence of external citations for high-level paradigm claims. Recurring markers of quality include verifiable incidents, consistent terminology, and the avoidance of false precision.
