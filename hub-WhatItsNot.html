WhatItsNot

This hub collects the positioning negatives — what aswritten is *not*. The load-bearing boundary is [search vs. aswritten] (Narrative_SearchVsAswritten): an AI can already web-search foreign policy, so the product \"doesn't replace search — it replaces the expert in the room\" (witnessed 2026-03-19), the difference being provenance, expert backing, and curated worldview. A second boundary handles the [ChatGPT-upload objection] (Obs_PlainLanguageDifferentiator) with a retained plain-language differentiator. A third, the [Apple/opinionated-workflow framing] (Obs_CoreThesisQuote2) (2026-03-26), defines it as a workflow with a conviction, *not a tool that does everything*. Underneath sits an explicit [retirement of the zero-context framing] (Obs_WhatItsNot_ZeroContext) — a road no longer taken. Three related style children — [the X-not-Y antithesis device] (StyleObs_NotPattern), [hybrid register] (StyleObs_8), and [first-person life's-work voice] (StyleObs_9) — describe *how* these boundaries get rhetorically sharpened rather than what they assert. The region narrates definition-by-exclusion across distinct competitor frames; it does not converge on a single one.
