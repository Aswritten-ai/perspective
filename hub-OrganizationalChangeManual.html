OrganizationalChangeManual

8 nodes in this area of the perspective

This hub gathers field notes toward a manual for organizational change in AI adoption — the recurring claim being that the bottleneck is human, not technical. [Org change as core friction] (Obs_OrgChangeManagement) and the [worldview-propagation thesis] (Claim_WorldviewPropagation) frame the problem: decisions trickle through management layers, killing projects on orders '6 months ago from someone 14 layers above you'. Two countervailing mechanics appear. [Democratized knowledge collection] (Narrative_DemocratizedConsensus), echoed in [Scarlet's framing] (Obs_DemocratizedConsensus), pushes representation *bottom-up* to blunt political influence. Against the usual product reflex, the customer's chief architect's [friction-as-feature logic] (Narrative_AdoptionIncentiveFrictionAsFeature) (May 2026, with [verbatim witness] (Obs_CustomerArchitectFrictionFeatureQuote)) argues friction should be *retained* for the AI-resistant cohort because struggle is the forcing function for fluency. That sits in direct tension with the [onboarding-gap claim] (Narrative_OnboardingGap) that the cognitive friction between connection and first value is the core problem to remove. A structurally distinct [personal-recovery note] (Observation_FiveYearWait) sits here too — rebuilt foundations, 'clean and sober' — reading more as founder backstory than org-change doctrine.
