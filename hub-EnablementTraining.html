This hub consolidates enablement and onboarding strategies for the aswritten beta, centering on the tension between user-led discovery and structured separation. Guidance is anchored by [Tony's technical-user separation principle] (Position_TechnicalUserSeparationPrinciple) (Mar 17), which mandates that users initiate fresh projects to avoid conflict with existing repositories—a stance reinforced by [his specific implementation advice] (Obs_SeparationSolutionQuote). This focus on isolation emerged partly from [prospect feedback] (Position_TighterOnboarding) (Mar 11) requesting clearer distinctions between documentation and AI-driven workflows. The onboarding experience is further shaped by [five recurring user questions] (Position_FiveQuestionsEveryUserAsks) and [Scarlet's first-use framing] (Obs_ChatNotCodingSession), which encourages treating the tool as a conversational partner rather than a traditional coding environment. Underlying these tactical choices is a conceptual distinction: [human-led onboarding] (Narrative_OnboardingEffectivenessVsCoverage) (Mar 13) is framed as a method to increase knowledge coverage rather than inherent product effectiveness, with [live bootstrapping guidance] (Obs_BootstrappingGuidance) serving as a precursor to future automated onboarding modes.
